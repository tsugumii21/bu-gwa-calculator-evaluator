About Bicol University GWA Calculator & Academic Evaluator

Learn more about the project, official policies, platform capabilities, and developer.

Bicol University GWA Calculator & Academic Evaluator

The Bicol University GWA Calculator is an independent, state-of-the-art web application specifically engineered for Bicol University students across all colleges and campuses. Built to eliminate manual calculation errors and complex spreadsheets, this tool empowers students to seamlessly compute 4-decimal General Weighted Averages (GWA), verify President's & Dean's Lister qualifications per semester, track graduation Latin Honors proximity, simulate future academic scenarios, and monitor scholarship retention compliance in real-time.

Download Official Student Handbook (PDF)
Official Academic Disclaimer & Governance

1. Independent Student Utility: The BU GWA Calculator & Academic Evaluator is an independent, non-official software utility created solely for reference, academic self-monitoring, and simulation purposes. It is not officially affiliated with, endorsed by, or operated by Bicol University administration.

2. Official Verification Authority: All outputs, computed averages, honor eligibility notifications, and scholarship status checks generated by this application remain strictly advisory. Official academic records, Dean's Lister / President's Lister certificates, graduation honors, and final transcripts remain subject to official verification and final confirmation by the Bicol University Office of the Registrar and respective College Deans.

3. Complete Privacy Guarantee: This application operates 100% client-side within your browser. All grades, course entries, and PDF Certificate of Registration (COR) extractions are processed locally on your device and stored in browser local storage. No academic data or personal information is ever transmitted to external servers.

Governing Bicol University Student Handbook Articles & Citations

Grading System & GWA
Article VI, Section 13–15 (Page 28)

Defines weighted grade point average computation `∑(Grade × Units) / ∑Units`, 4-decimal rounding, and non-numerical mark exclusions (INC/DRP) per BOR Res. 89 s. 2006.

Term Honors (PL & DL)
Article VIII, Section 28–29 (Page 34–35)

Establishes President's Lister (1.0000–1.4500, max grade 1.75) and Dean's Lister (1.4600–1.7500, max grade 2.50) semester honor roll cutoffs and grade caps.

Graduation Latin Honors
Article VIII, Section 30 (Page 36)

Defines Summa Cum Laude (1.0000–1.2500), Magna Cum Laude (1.2501–1.4500), and Cum Laude (1.4501–1.7500) thresholds and full-load residence rules.

Underloaded Term Policy
Article VIII, Section 30 (Page 36)

States that enrolling in fewer units than prescribed in the regular curriculum officially disqualifies a candidate from graduation honors and semester recognition.

Scholastic Academic Standing
Article VII, Section 21–24 (Page 31–32)

Outlines Good Standing (0 deficiencies), Academic Warning (1 fail), Academic Probation (2 fails - 75% load cap), and Academic Dismissal Risk (3+ fails).

Academic Credit Load
Article V, Section 8–10 (Page 22–24)

Regulates semester credit unit loading, pre-requisite course validation, and full-time student status requirements.

Core Platform Capabilities

4-Decimal Precision Engine

Computes real-time cumulative and semester GWAs using exact weighted grade point math rounded to 4 decimal places per official university policy.

Digital COR PDF Scanner

Instant client-side extraction of course codes, descriptions, and credit units directly from official BU PDF documents via PDF.js worker.

Honor Qualification Radar

Evaluates President's Lister, Dean's Lister, and graduation Latin Honors (Summa, Magna, Cum Laude) while showing exact point proximity gaps.

Scenario Simulator

Simulate future grade outcomes across remaining units and reverse-calculate exact required average grades to achieve target graduation honors.
